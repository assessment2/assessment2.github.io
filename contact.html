Software Test Analyst

Field requires subject to verify and validate applications within a controlled environment, to ensure that they meet all requirements, whilst being screened for bugs and errors before they are implemented or delivered to an end user, ensuring all facets of the product function as intended. 

Work Interactions: Subject is required to frequently liaise and coordinate tasks with the software developers. Communication is paramount to this role thus it is common for Test Analysts to coordinate with all involved departments, such as designers if there are issues with the GUI. Transparency between departments and the test analyst  is key in order for the tested methods employed to be appropriate for the software and ensure that every potential point of failure is examined. Throughout the testing process, communication continues between all departments involved, until a final report of findings is submitted so that bugs can be addressed. Upon which, the software is retested.

Time investment: Communication within this field is paramount, thus subject will often find themselves attending meetings with the heads of different departments to address issues that they or the department may be facing, as well as to discuss the test report findings. Whilst most communication is carried out via email and VoIP, physical meetings are also frequently held. Although Test Analysts often work within their own environment, physical meetings may also be attended if necessary. 

Challenging aspects: One of the most commonly faced dilemmas within this field is lacking test documentation. This occurs due to the dependence of most developers on verbal communication, however without documentation, key facets of the software may be left unexplored thus increasing the potential for bugs to exist, inhibiting the software’s functionality and the end user’s experience. Complications may also arise in the face of poor coordination between different departments. Given that the final product is generally the combined effort of multiple departments, a lack of transparency may prompt a situation where the software being examined is missing key components, thus the test analyst will be required to conduct additional testing that should not have been necessary.

Director of Web Development

Time investment: Given that communication is carried out primarily over VoIP, the subject is able to base themselves within their own workspace, unless physical meetings are required.

Challenging aspects: Challenges within this field arise in the form of having to negotiate between multiple parties with differing ideas, ensuring adequate progress is made. Autonomous Vehicles

Hamish Prasad: Autonomous Vehicles, more commonly referred to as driverless cars, aim to offer users an automated means of transport that requires little physical interaction from the driver, in a manner which prioritises road safety and efficiency. This rapidly advancing innovation bears the potential to revolutionize transport within contemporary society, particularly through alleviating road congestion, even within it’s advent. Autonomous vehicles utilize a series of multiple sensors, most notably RADAR and LiDAR, which acquire positional data concerning the vehicle’s surroundings, including other automobiles, physical boundaries, lane markings and pedestrians (Ondruš, Kolla, Vertaľ and Šarić, 2020). This data is interpreted and analysed in real time by onboard predictive AI systems, and utilized in order to automate steering wheel position, braking and acceleration. 

Currently, the automated driving capabilities of such vehicles are characterised by a tier system (Chan, 2017), with L1 featuring a barebones system that can monitor acceleration and maintain cruise speed controlling of speed increments can be handled without human intervention, however most functionality remains completely human controlled, L2 being partial automation of both steering and acceleration whilst still requiring human monitoring, L3 indicating conditional automation, the ability for the AI to monitor changes to the environment and perform all basic driving needs, L4 reflecting the car’s ability to adapt if there is a system failure or sudden obstacles, make decisions and drive safely without necessarily requiring human intervention. In any case, it very well might be altered not to work in unmapped zones or during extreme environments. L5 is characterised by the vehicle’s ability to monitor and drive without any human interaction, carrying out all tasks and eliminating the need for a steering wheel, acceleration, braking, and other manual controls (Zhao, Liang and Chen, 2018). Such technology is available more abundantly in the form of self parking cars, which detect the marked boundaries of an assigned parking space, and calculate the safest and most efficient means of parking the car within the designated space. Consumers have been granted a glimpse at the capabilities of this technology through current commercial offerings, however given safety concerns, mass adoption of driverless cars throughout society has yet to occur. That being said, the autonomous vehicle market is rapidly growing, currently worth over $54 billion, and estimated to reach $540 billion by 2026 (Jadhav, 2019). 

Current state of the art implementations, most notably Tesla’s Model S (Autopilot - L2), Cadillac’s CT6 (Super Cruise - L2) and Audi’s A8 (L3), are made possible with predictive AI and imaging applications (Kurczewski, 2020). RADAR and LiDAR sensors collect images in real time of the vehicle’s surroundings and positional data, enabling the software to position the car within a virtual 3D space. Obstructions, lane markings and the movement data of surrounding vehicles or pedestrians, assist the AI in making decisions that determine the car’s movement. The current goal of today’s state of the art autonomous vehicle manufacturers is to reduce the latency between the acquisition of images by the sensors, and completion of the AI’s interpretation, to zero. As of now, L2 autonomous cars have successfully exhibited the ability to maintain cruising speeds, park, and halt in the case of obstruction (Chan, 2017).

It is expected that in time, more mainstream car manufacturers will find a means to overcome the current imaging concerned technological boundaries that prevent L3 tier vehicles from being produced on a mass scale (Zhao, Liang and Chen, 2018). Current imaging technologies, particularly RADAR, video, and LiDAR sensors, are plagued by issues with focus, visibility through fog, and inhibited depth perception. However, research into thermal imaging sensors has provided a means for such limitations to be addressed, given that they detect frequencies of light on a much broader scale than the visual spectrum, proving to be effective in environmental conditions where video feed falls short, as exhibited by FLIR System’s recent imaging sensors (Cannon, 2019). Thus autonomous vehicle technology may advance towards the mass implementation of thermal imaging, with RADAR, LiDAR and video feed as secondary sensors. 

Almost 1.3 million individuals die in vehicle crashes across the world each year, which makes it around 4,000 passings each day (Road traffic injuries, Fagnant, 2017). Many fatalities occur exclusively because of carelessness, rash driving, being affected by intoxicating substances, or other avoidable human blunders. Subsequently, vehicle protection is a multi-billion dollar business. Yet, when there is not a human driver behind the guiding, and the vehicle would explore environments with the assistance of LiDAR and AI calculations, the likelihood of vehicle accidents would be insignificant. Furthermore, this would bring about fewer individuals choosing protections and, in the end, lead to insurance agencies bearing substantial misfortunes.

MIT analysts anticipate that autonomous vehicles would impact where individuals decide to live (Bissell, Birtchnell, Elliott and Hsu, 2018). Driving can be a horrendous ordeal for some, particularly when they need to invest a lot of energy out and about in the pains of traffic. In any case, individuals can live a long way from their work environments with fully autonomous vehicles. Commutes will be more bearable since the time generally spent driving can be diverted into other productive activities. Another huge manner by which autonomous vehicles can affect contemporary society is the decision of self-reliant living. For the older, debilitated, self-driving vehicles will tremendously influence their lives since travelling via car will no longer be limited by the individual’s physical capacity to operate a car manually, empowering outwardly disabled individuals (Bissell, Birtchnell, Elliott and Hsu, 2018).

Andreas Tschiesner depicts the likely reality of autonomous vehicles having a substantial impact on the economy. He goes further in his exploration to say that there will be a decrease in transport and train drivers and government employees who work in broad daylight public transport roles and traffic divisions. While this may look like negative news, Tschiesner provides some solace by expressing that the implementation of autonomous vehicles in urban communities will prompt an ascent in the demand for researchers and engineers to help govern and maintain both the vehicles themselves, and the way in which society functions around them (Tschiesner, 2018). Discourse regarding the safety of the mass implementation of self driving vehicles can also be anticipated, given that the technology is arguably still in its infancy, with L4-L5 tier autonomous cars only just being developed. Many individuals may feel unsafe to have to share roads with vehicles that are being driven autonomously, and thus may show disapproval of their commercial introduction to the market (Schwalb, 2021).       

The impact of L3-L5 tier autonomous vehicles on contemporary society, will extend further into the personal lives of not only myself but those around me, given that I have a family member who is unable to operate a car due to a physical disability. A fully autonomous mode of transportation would grant individuals facing that scenario with unprecedented independence, no longer needing to rely on others to travel. This independence may also offer vast benefits towards mental health. Within my personal life, implementation of such developments would enable me to no longer rely on public transport for daily commutes, thus lowering the risk of potentially contracting an illness, given that I am immunocompromised and do not currently hold a full license, ruling out the opportunity for me to drive a car myself.

Robotics


The field of robotics has long undergone rapid advancements, most notably since the advent the 21st century. The field can be considered the amalgamation of developments in both engineering and science to create programmable or autonomous machinery with the intent to imitate physical human actions and traits, and perform actions humans can not Hu, H., 2012. Robotics. Early innovations in robotics aided in monotonous tasks, particularly robotic arms designed for use in automobile assembly lines. However, recent advances have granted robots to carry out increasingly complex tasks including bomb defusal, handling fires, as well as enabling surgeons to carry out intricate maneuvers with unprecedented accuracy. Robots have been designed with varying capacities for autonomous function, from pre programmable robots that require human commands, to fully autonomous creations that can make their own judgement based on machine learning without human interaction Hu, H., 2012. Robotics. 


One current state of the art application of robotics that is currently used is robot-assisted surgery Satava, R., 2004. This has granted doctors to perform many difficult surgical maneuvers with unprecedented precision and accuracy, to degrees higher than what was previously capable. Typically, robot-assisted surgery involves the use of a camera, viewed by the surgeon, and multiple arms equipped with various surgical tools. This application of robotics requires the surgeon to directly control the arms, however, it is considered state of the art as it grants the doctor with a magnified view of the subject, enabling precision at a level that ensures the patient’s well being during recovery to a significantly higher degree Satava, R., 2004. 


Presently, robotics can be classified by five main categories, Humanoid, Programmed, Autonomous, Teleoperated and Augmenting. A notable application of this technology can be seen in automotive assembly lines. The robot can perform far more efficiently than a human, with increased precision, these are generally applied in scenarios involving welding, insertion of sensitive components into difficult areas such as the engine. These mechanical arms can perform tasks significantly faster than humans. Robots that mimic human behavior and appearance have also been developed and are typically labelled Humanoid robots. They boast the ability to carry objects, walk and sprint, and often times feature human like faces that can show expression. The most notable innovator in this sector is Boston dynamics, with their “Atlas”, a robot designed to carry out tasks in environments deemed too harsh for humans, without supervision. They utilize a multitude of sensors, including thermal, lidar and radar, to monitor 3D surroundings in real time, which is then processed with machine learning, enabling the robot to make decisions that determine how the robot should interact with the surrounding environment. A notable application of autonomous robotic technology can be seen with the Roomba. A robotic, self operating vacuum cleaner that senses it’s surroundings, enabling it to make movement decisions within a home. Semi-autonomous robots are typically still human controlled, but can be operated from greater distances, enabling humans to not be physically present within their vicinity. These robots are generally employed to work within dangerous conditions. Semi-autonomous robots have been deployed to work at vast depths within the ocean to service pipes, or to investigate suspicious packages that could contain explosives. The last category of robots, dubbed augmenting robots, are designed to enhance existing human functions or re-grant them to individuals who may have lost certain physical abilities. These robotic developments can grant humans greater speed, agility and strength. An example of this application today are prosthetic limbs given to amputees, or hydraulic-assisted arms that grant users strength.


Within the next 3 years, an increased adoption of AI machine learning into robotics is anticipated, further granting robots the ability make their own guided and non guided decision concerning the tasks assigned, whilst taking into account their surrounding environment with sensor technology Raj, M., Seamans, R. Primer on artificial intelligence and robotics. J Org Design 8, 11 (2019). 


One key technological development prompting the advancement of robotics is the adoption and incorporation of AI and machine learning. Currently robotic machines that utilize machine learning boast the ability to scan their environment to recognize particular objects irrespective of their location. Machine learning has also granted robots the ability to learn how to deal with unfamiliar circumstances, particularly handling foreign objects. AI has also proven to be beneficial to movement technology, allowing robots to make decisions concerning movement in unfamiliar or dynamic terrains, thus granting them the ability to perform tasks that may be dangerous to humans. 


Along with its praise, several negative impacts have also been anticipated from the future advancement and mass implementation of robotic devices. This primarily manifests within the concern for job loss due to AI. As of now, many jobs centered around manual labour have already been carried out by robots, replacing jobs that were once carried out by humans. It is feared that in the future, only jobs that require creativity or analytical decision making will remain. Research conducted by B. McKinsey suggests that by 2030 one third of all jobs involving manual labour may be replaced by automated machinery, thus it is expected to significantly disrupt the job market.  However it is also anticipated that many jobs will be created, for such machinery must also be designed and maintained, as well as providing a new platform for innovation.
